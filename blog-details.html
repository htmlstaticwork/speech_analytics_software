What a “negative spike at 2:34” really means

A sentiment chart is not a vanity metric. In a call center, spikes are operational evidence: a policy mismatch, a hold-heavy workflow, a missing explanation, or an agent behavior that increases escalation risk.

Start with the timestamp, not the average

Averages hide the only thing that matters: the moment the customer changes. Use timestamps to jump directly to the trigger, then label it as a repeatable driver.

Four common drivers supervisors can coach

Long hold or dead air

Interruptions and pacing

Turn the driver into a coaching action

The goal is not to “improve sentiment.” The goal is to reduce escalations, control AHT, and increase First Call Resolution by coaching specific behaviors: hold scripts, empathy phrases, pacing, and clear next steps.
